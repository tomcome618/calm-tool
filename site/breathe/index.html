Common Questions

What is the 4-7-8 breathing technique? expand_more
The 4-7-8 technique is a pranayama breathing exercise that involves inhaling for 4 seconds, holding the breath for 7 seconds, and exhaling forcefully for 8 seconds. It acts as a natural tranquilizer for the nervous system.
How often should I practice box breathing? expand_more
Box breathing can be practiced anytime you feel stressed or need to regain focus. Many professionals use it for 3-5 minutes before high-stakes meetings or difficult tasks to reset their physiological state.
Can breathing exercises help with anxiety? expand_more
Yes, deep breathing activates the parasympathetic nervous system, which signals the brain to relax. This can lower your heart rate, reduce cortisol levels, and help manage physical symptoms of anxiety.
Is it normal to feel lightheaded? expand_more
If you're new to deep breathing, mild lightheadedness can occur due to increased oxygen intake. If you feel dizzy, return to normal breathing immediately. Always practice in a safe, seated position.
Why are longer exhales emphasized in 'Simple Calm'? expand_more
Exhaling for longer than you inhale directly stimulates the Vagus nerve, which is the primary component of your parasympathetic nervous system. This is one of the fastest ways to force the body out of 'fight or flight' mode.
