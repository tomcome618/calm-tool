Breathing Fundamentals

What is the 4-7-8 breathing technique? expand_more

Developed by Dr. Andrew Weil, 4-7-8 is a rhythmic pattern that acts as a natural tranquilizer for the nervous system. By holding the breath and extending the exhale, you signal to your brain that it is safe to down-regulate the "fight or flight" response.

How often should I practice box breathing? expand_more

Navy SEALs use box breathing for immediate stress management. For long-term benefits, try practicing for 5 minutes twice a day, or whenever you feel a spike in anxiety or physiological tension.

Can breathing exercises help with anxiety? expand_more

Yes. Deep, conscious breathing increases oxygen levels in the blood and stimulates the vagus nerve, which tells the body to calm down. It is one of the fastest biological ways to reduce acute anxiety.

Is it normal to feel lightheaded? expand_more

Occasional lightheadedness is common for beginners as the body adjusts to higher oxygen intake. If you feel dizzy, stop the exercise and breathe normally for a few minutes. Always practice in a seated or lying position.

Why are longer exhales emphasized in Simple Calm? expand_more

The exhale is linked to the parasympathetic nervous system (the "rest and digest" mode). By making the exhale longer than the inhale, you prioritize relaxation over stimulation.
